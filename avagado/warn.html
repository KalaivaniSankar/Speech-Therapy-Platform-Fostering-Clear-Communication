Articulation Disorder

Description: Articulation disorder involves difficulty in producing sounds and sound sequences correctly due to incorrect placement, timing, pressure, speed, or flow of movement of the lips, tongue, or throat.

Stages: Identification, Evaluation, Treatment

Warnings: Delayed language development, difficulty being understood by others

Activities: Speech therapy exercises focusing on correct sound production, tongue twisters

Practices: Practice speaking slowly and clearly, practice making individual sounds

Phonology Disorder

Description: Phonology disorder involves difficulty in understanding and using the sound system of a language, resulting in errors in speech sounds and sound patterns.

Stages: Identification, Assessment, Intervention

Warnings: Difficulty with reading and spelling, trouble communicating effectively

Activities: Sound discrimination activities, rhyming games

Practices: Listening to and imitating correct speech sounds, practicing sound patterns

Fluency Disorder

Description: Fluency disorder involves disruptions in the normal flow of speech, characterized by repetitions, prolongations, or blocks of sounds, syllables, words, or phrases.

Stages: Identification, Assessment, Intervention

Warnings: Anxiety related to speaking situations, impact on social interactions

Activities: Speech therapy techniques like slow speech and easy onset, relaxation exercises

Practices: Practice speaking slowly and with reduced tension, gradual increase of speaking rate

Voice Disorder

Description: Voice disorder involves abnormalities in pitch, volume, or quality of the voice, which may result from structural or functional issues in the vocal folds or other parts of the vocal tract.

Stages: Identification, Diagnosis, Treatment

Warnings: Hoarseness, vocal fatigue, difficulty projecting voice

Activities: Vocal exercises, breathing exercises, vocal hygiene practices

Practices: Avoiding behaviors that strain the voice, staying hydrated, avoiding irritants

Language Skills Disorder

Description: Language skills disorder involves difficulties in understanding and using language, including vocabulary, grammar, and comprehension, which may affect communication in various contexts.

Stages: Identification, Evaluation, Intervention

Warnings: Difficulty following instructions, delayed language milestones

Activities: Language therapy activities, storytelling, language games

Practices: Encouraging language-rich environments, providing clear and concise instructions

Social Communication Disorder

Description: Social communication disorder involves challenges in using verbal and nonverbal communication for social purposes, leading to difficulties in social interactions and relationships.

Stages: Identification, Assessment, Intervention

Warnings: Difficulty understanding social cues, trouble initiating or maintaining conversations

Activities: Social skills training, role-playing exercises, conversation practice

Practices: Encouraging peer interactions, providing visual supports for communication
